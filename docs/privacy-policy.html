RemoteFlow Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

Overview

RemoteFlow is an SSH client application. Your privacy is important to us. This policy explains what data RemoteFlow accesses and how it is handled.

Data Storage

All data is stored locally on your device. RemoteFlow does not operate any servers and does not transmit your data to any RemoteFlow-operated infrastructure.

  • Host configurations (hostname, port, username) are stored in local on-device storage.
  • SSH credentials (passwords, private keys, passphrases) are stored exclusively in your device's secure storage — iOS Keychain on Apple devices and Android Keystore on Android devices. These credentials are never stored in plain text.
  • Session history and app settings are stored locally on-device.

Network Communications

RemoteFlow connects only to the SSH servers you configure. The app:

  • Does not connect to any RemoteFlow servers
  • Does not send analytics or telemetry data
  • Does not use third-party analytics services
  • Does not use advertising networks
  • Does not use cookies or tracking technologies

Third-Party Data Sharing

RemoteFlow does not share any data with third parties. No personal data, usage data, or credentials are transmitted outside your device except to the SSH servers you explicitly connect to.

SSH Encryption

All SSH connections use industry-standard encryption protocols. RemoteFlow uses the dartssh2 library which implements SSH-2 protocol with standard cipher suites. This encryption qualifies for mass-market exemption under BIS Export Administration Regulations (EAR).

Data Deletion

You can delete all stored data at any time by:

  • Deleting individual hosts from the connection list
  • Uninstalling the application (removes all local data)

Children's Privacy

RemoteFlow is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Changes will be reflected in the "Last updated" date above.

Contact

For questions about this privacy policy, please open an issue on the RemoteFlow GitHub repository.
